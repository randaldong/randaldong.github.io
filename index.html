1. General Gameplay Design

1.1 Overall Mission

Two players are placed at separate scenes. They have to collaborate to solve puzzles and find clues in the scene, helping each other to collect the missing parts of the ancient stone tablet that opens the door to the next unknown……

1.2 Character Setting

  • Player 1 (Apolonia, relating to Apollo, representing rational thinking and order, logic and reason, prudence and discipline)
  • Player 2 (Dion, relating to Dionysus, representing irrationality, chaos, passion, emotions, and instincts)

(from Friedrich Nietzsche § Apollonian and Dionysian in The Birth of Tragedy)

In this game, these two spirits are inextricably entwined by the force of nature. Though players are initially separated into different scenes, they must unite as a team, collaborating to gather useful information and solve puzzles by interacting with their surroundings. Once they have located all of the ancient tablet fragments scattered throughout their surroundings, and pieced them together in their proper place, the ornately carved wooden door shall open before them, revealing the culmination of their collective efforts. At this moment, they shall witness the magnificent merging of their memories, previously disparate droplets, into a single, continuous river, restoring their fractured spirits to wholeness once more.

1.3 Task Setting

1.3.1 Apolonia’s Tasks

Apolonia’s Tasks are more related to logic. She needs to communicate with Dion frequently to get clues so that she can finish all tasks and get three “keys" to pass the cat keeper. Interaction design for Apolonia would be focused on selection & manipulation.

1.3.2 Dion’s Tasks

Dion’s Tasks are more related to arts. He is the drifter of a world full of imagination and fantasy, and sometimes illusion and absurdity. Optical illusions and surrealism ideas would be used for the task design of Dion.

4. Puzzle Design

4.1 Morse Code + Boat Race in Chinese Shan Shui

An unfurled ancient scroll is on the left side of the desk, with puzzles written on it. A Morse code alphabet with letters and numbers is on the right side of the desk. An input area is on the bottom side of the desk. Apolonia needs to solve the puzzle, encode the answer to Morse code, and input the sequence of dots and dashes into the empty slots to obtain the tools at the next level.

4.2 Balance Scale + Mondriaan+ Nighthawks Universe

The balance scale ⚖️ follows the Subtractive Color Mixing 🎨 (such as in a printer) rule: it will be balanced only if weights on the two sides are equal to each other in terms of color. All conditions are listed as follows:

  1. cyan + yellow = green
  2. cyan + magenta = blue
  3. yellow + magenta = red
  4. cyan + yellow + magenta = black

Initially, Apolonia can see an instruction on the left side of the desk, explaining color mixing and scale constraint (she can put only 1 weight on the left of the scale and 2 weights on the right); a red weight on the left of the scale; and six other weights (yellow, green, cyan, blue, magenta, black) aside; Dion can see pieces of glass at the center of the Mondriaan scene (as the above picture on the right but only red ones show up) together with four doors that have different colors (red, white, green, black). Clues about the next color Apolonia should put on the scale and “weigh” are hidden in the poem on the scroll. The whole process will be:

  1. Apolonia is asked to tell Dion that she saw a red weight on the left of the scale and Dion should find a clue related to this color.

  1. So Dion enter the scene of Nighthawks through the red door. He should find a red scroll in the scene. The red scroll says:

"The yellow fog that rubs its back upon the window-panes,

The yellow smoke that rubs its muzzle on the window-panes,

Licked its tongue into the corners of the evening,

Lingered upon the pools that stand in drains,

Let fall upon its back the soot that falls from chimneys,

Slipped by the terrace, made a sudden leap,

And seeing that it was a soft October night,

Curled once about the house, and fell asleep."

—T. S. Eliot, "The Love Song of J. Alfred Prufrock"

So, Dion tells Apolonia that the clue is yellow. After Dion rolls up (close) the red scroll, he will be teleported to the Mondriaan scene and the yellow pieces of glass will show up.

  1. Then, Apolonia knows that she should put yellow and magenta weights on the right side of the scale. Once the scale becomes balanced, a verse will appear on the desk, saying:

“Whitespace is to be regarded as an active element, not a passive background”

— Jan Tschichold

Apolonia tells Dion that the clue is white, so Dion knows that he should choose the white door this time and find a white scroll, which says:

“Angels of rain and lightning: there are spread

On the blue surface of thine airy surge,

Like the bright hair uplifted from the head”

—Percy Bysshe Shelley, "Ode to the West Wind", Canto 2, Stanza 2

Dion should tell Apolonia that the clue is blue. After Dion rolls up (close) the white scroll, he will be teleported to the Mondriaan scene and the blue pieces of glass will show up.

  1. Having obtained the clue, Apolonia knows that she should replace red with blue, yellow with cyan. Once the scale becomes balanced again, a riddle appears on the desk:

“I will not cease from mental fight,

Nor shall my sword sleep in my hand,

Till we have built Jerusalem

In England's green and pleasant land.”

—William Blake, "And Did Those Feet In Ancient Time"

Apolonia should tell Dion that the clue is green.

  1. Dion walks through the green door. Under the spotlight in the bar, he will find the green scroll. As the scroll unfurled, Dion finds out that the inside of the scroll is entirely black. Dion tells Apolonia what he saw (including both green and black). After getting the information, Apolonia knows that now she should put black on the left; green and blue on the right. After Dion rolls up (close) the green scroll, he will be teleported to the Mondriaan scene and the green pieces of glass will show up.

  1. Then, the Mondriaan cube starts spinning, faster and faster. Suddenly, Dion is transferred to the new scene: Van Gogh’s Cafe Terrace at Night, but it’s silent and lifeless. While on the side of Apolonia, once the scale becomes balanced again, all the objects on this desk will disappear. Tools that will be manipulated at the next level will show up on the net desk.

5. Summary

Uniduality: A Unique Offline Multiplayer Adventure

Embark on a thrilling gaming experience unlike any other! Uniduality is an immersive two-player game that breaks away from the conventional online multiplayer format. Say goodbye to endless network synchronization and embrace a truly engaging offline adventure.

In Uniduality, effective communication becomes the key to success. Engage with your teammate in the physical world, utilizing voice calls or face-to-face interaction, to uncover clues and confirm each other's state. This groundbreaking feature not only adds an exciting layer of realism but also fosters genuine interpersonal communication. Strengthen the bond with your teammate as you conquer challenges together, relying on natural language to strategize and conquer the game's obstacles.

But that's not all. Uniduality goes beyond the boundaries of traditional gaming. By playing together physically, you unlock the power of true physical interaction. Break free from the limitations of virtual reality's lack of feedback and experience a game where your actions have tangible consequences. Immerse yourself in a world where your physical presence truly matters, adding an extra layer of excitement and realism to your gaming journey.

Are you ready to embark on an extraordinary adventure, where communication, cooperation, and physical interaction merge to create an unforgettable gaming experience? Uniduality awaits!
